Formulas & Review Notes

Ohm's Law

V = I × R
I = V / R
R = V / I

V = Voltage (Volts, V) · I = Current (Amperes, A) · R = Resistance (Ohms, Ω)

Electrical Power

P = V × I
P = I² × R
P = V² / R

P = Power (Watts, W). Power is the rate of energy consumption or delivery.

Energy & Work

W = P × t
W = V × I × t
kWh = (W × h) / 1000

W = Energy (Joules), t = time (seconds). Kilowatt-hour is a unit of electrical energy.

Series Circuit

R_T = R₁ + R₂ + R₃ …
I_T = I₁ = I₂ = I₃
V_T = V₁ + V₂ + V₃

Current is same everywhere. Voltage divides. Total resistance = sum of all resistors.

Parallel Circuit

1/R_T = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ …
V_T = V₁ = V₂ = V₃
I_T = I₁ + I₂ + I₃

Voltage is same across all branches. Current divides. Total resistance is always less than the smallest.

Two Resistors Parallel

R_T = (R₁ × R₂) / (R₁ + R₂)

The product-over-sum shortcut — only works for exactly 2 resistors in parallel.

Voltage Divider Rule

V_x = V_T × (R_x / R_T)

Used in series circuits to find voltage across one resistor when total voltage is known.

Current Divider Rule

I₁ = I_T × R₂ / (R₁+R₂)
I₂ = I_T × R₁ / (R₁+R₂)

For two parallel resistors: more current flows through the smaller resistor.

Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL)

ΣI_in = ΣI_out

Sum of currents entering a node equals sum leaving. Charge is conserved.

Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL)

ΣV = 0 (around loop)

Sum of all voltages around a closed loop is zero. Energy is conserved.

Conductance

G = 1 / R
Unit: Siemens (S)

Conductance is the reciprocal of resistance. Higher conductance → easier current flow.

Resistivity

R = ρ × L / A

ρ = resistivity (Ω·m), L = length (m), A = cross-sectional area (m²). Longer wire = more resistance. Thicker wire = less resistance.

Unit Prefixes

  • k (kilo) = × 1,000
  • M (mega) = × 1,000,000
  • m (milli) = × 0.001
  • μ (micro) = × 0.000001
  • n (nano) = × 10⁻⁹

Key Relationships

  • ↑ Resistance → ↓ Current (same V)
  • ↑ Voltage → ↑ Current (same R)
  • Series adds resistance
  • Parallel reduces resistance
  • Power = Heat dissipated
Resistor Color Code

How to Read a Resistor (4-Band)

Value = (Band1 × 10 + Band2) × Multiplier ± Tolerance%

Band 1 = 1st digit · Band 2 = 2nd digit · Band 3 = Multiplier · Band 4 = Tolerance

5-band: (B1×100 + B2×10 + B3) × Multiplier ± Tolerance
